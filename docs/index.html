Onboarding workflows

Three ways to join the collaboration layer with enough trust, clarity, and protection to make the work useful.


Mentor profile

  1. 1Create a profile.
  2. 2Explain why founders should believe you can help.
  3. 3Describe how you can support.
  4. 4Choose the areas where you can support.
  5. 5Link GitHub to show credibility.
  6. 6Share what you like to support, because passion is key.

Founder path

  1. 1Create a profile and tell us your drive and motivation.
  2. 2Choose a restricted starting area.
  3. 3Explain the problem you want to solve and why.
  4. 4Describe who benefits and who the user is.
  5. 5Explain how you want to solve it.
  6. 6Add a short video or presentation.
  7. 7Choose public, semi-private, or private review mode.
  8. 8Tell us what help and support you need.
  9. 9Tell us if you want to start building a team.
  10. 10Improve from feedback and see your progress over time.

Participant profile

  1. 1Create a profile.
  2. 2Describe how you can support.
  3. 3Choose the areas where you can support.
  4. 4Link GitHub to show credibility.
  5. 5Share what you like to support, because passion is key.

What ships in the MVP

  • Next.js app with Prisma, PostgreSQL, NextAuth, Zod, React Hook Form, and Tailwind.
  • Role-aware founder, reviewer, expert, and moderation workspaces.
  • Asset visibility, disclosure requests, access logs, moderation cases, badges, and reputation events.
  • Seeded demo data covering users, categories, projects, versions, reviews, expert assessments, and leaderboards.
